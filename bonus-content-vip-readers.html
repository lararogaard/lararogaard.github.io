Hades A full moon shines down on us as we walk on the seedy streets of the Bronx. “We don’t have time for this. We need a breakthrough. They’re hot on our heels,” I say to Ares as he walks by my side. “This guy is our best bet. Everything he told us checked out, right Thanatos?” Ares asks, turning his head towards him. “So far,” says Thanatos, his face buried in his cell. The absent tone echoes his phantasmal appearance, as if light from his phone screen had possessed him. I exhale all my hopelessness with force, as if doing so would exorcise it from my soul. In the chilly night, the white dance of my breath is a specter who assumes her delicate form, her flowing hair, her full lips calling my name in dreams. The vision transports me to a distant land, filled with warmth and ripe fruit-laden fields, where the sun caresses us during the day and the moon guards us at night. If only I could find her—
